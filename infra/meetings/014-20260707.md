### Meeting notes

#### Expanding the meeting to more participants
  We would like to incentivize attending meetings. Christian suggests that consistency and public referencing of the meeting may help. Andreas suggests looking into LFS features (e.g. mailing lists), but is not convinced that this would help. Xavier attests that he joined today because of recent communications and the recently published Roadmap.
  
#### Roadmap discussion
   Andreas explains the further context for the recently created Roadmaps. The primary driver was the Pytorch foundation application, but Andreas is also of the opinion that it is helpful to  know what the different groups are working on / planning. Prior experience shows that many people make small, individual contributions, but the larger picture is unclear. 
   Xavier gives context about the convert roadmap and makes the case for a mono-repo-convert project. Xavier's team is focused mainly on LLMs rather than converter work these days. They use many converters outside the PyTorch package. According to Xavier, pytorch converter tooling is very active, but the other converter projects see less activity.
   
#### Onboarding new maintainers for TF converter package
  Andreas reports that there are two new potential maintainers for the TensorFlow converters. Andreas asks how to onboard them. Xavier thinks the current TF-converter project is very hard to maintain and favors a reboot. Andreas suggests discussing this further in the converter SIG meeting and inviting the potential new maintainers there.
  
#### Symbolic shape inference
   Xavier tells of his symbolic shape inference experiments. Christian suggests updating the existing RFC (https://github.com/onnx/onnx/pull/7692) or creating a new one _if_ necessary.
   
#### Convert SIG meeting
   Andreas offers to help Xavier with setting up the converter sig meeting in the calendar.

#### Projects in the ONNX namespace
   Christian and Andreas went through some seemingly abandoned projects in the onnx GitHub namespace, which might be associated with the Infra-SIG. TurnkeyML appears to have an active successor. Digestai seems stalled. Christian is in favor of more aggressively archiving (not deleting) these repositories. Andreas voiced a preference to notify previous contributors of these projects again before possibly archiving them.
